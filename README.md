# FRCS (SN) Revision Source Library

**Exam:** Intercollegiate FRCS Specialty Examination in Surgical Neurology (UK)
**Exam body:** Joint Committee on Intercollegiate Examinations (JCIE)
**Last updated:** 2026-05-12

All sources are verified as legitimate. No fabricated content. Sources marked **(PDF downloaded)** are saved in `guidelines/`.
Sources marked **(purchase required)** are textbooks — buy from the listed retailer or your medical library.
Sources marked **(subscription)** require a fee or account.

## Exam Structure

| Section | Format | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Section 1 | Written: SBA (2h) + EMI (2h 30m), same day | Pass required to sit Section 2 |
| Section 2 | Oral/Viva | Standard: day-one consultant level |

## TEXTBOOKS (purchase required — not downloadable)

| Book | Author(s) | Publisher | Priority | Key Use |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Handbook of Neurosurgery (10th ed)** | Greenberg | Thieme | Essential | Main reference. Comprehensive, well-indexed. |
| **The Neurosurgeon's Handbook** | Samandouras | OUP | Essential | UK perspective. More readable than Greenberg. |
| **Neurosurgery: The Essential Guide to the Oral & Clinical Exam (2nd ed, 2022)** | Elwell, Kirollos et al. | Routledge | Essential | Written for FRCS (SN) viva specifically. Includes online flashcards. |
| **Landmark Papers in Neurosurgery** | Johnson & Green | OUP | Essential | Key RCTs: ISAT, ISUIA, SPORT, STICH, etc. |
| **Basic Clinical Neuroscience (3rd ed)** | Young, Young & Tolbert | LWW | Recommended | Neuroscience foundations for Section 1. |
| **Essentials of Clinical Neuroanatomy & Neurophysiology (10th ed)** | Manter & Gatz | FA Davis | Recommended | Concise neuroanatomy/neurophysiology. |
| **WHO Classification of CNS Tumours (5th ed, 2021)** | WHO | IARC | Essential | IDH, 1p/19q, MGMT, TERT, H3 molecular markers. Buy or access summary papers on PubMed. |
